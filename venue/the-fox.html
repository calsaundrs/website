The Fox

17 Lower Essex St, Birmingham, B5 6SN

LGBTQ+Venue

About The Fox

One of Birmingham's oldest and most loved LGBTQ+ pubs; Traditional Victorian pub; Warm and welcoming atmosphere; Particular favourite with the lesbian community, but inclusive to all.

What you'll find

🍸 Cocktails 🍺 Beer 🍷 Wine 🎤 Live music ☀️ Outdoor seating 👯 Good for groups

The Fox Reviews

Looking for reviews of The Fox? Here is what people are saying about this Birmingham venue.

Paul Nightingale

We love the Fox! It’s such a friendly and welcoming place for everyone. The staff are lovely people - approachable, kind and friendly. They run a lot of interesting and unique community events, and provide a safe space for people to be fully themselves. As well as the atmosphere, it has some really cool artwork, a great outdoor area, and reasonably priced drinks. Absolutely recommend stopping by if you’re in the area.

Alison Kelly

Walked into this bar alone not knowing anyone as was made to feel like part of the team. The owner made sure I was taken care of and his staff were so nice . Big shout out to Kitty and Daz so welcoming. Atmosphere was electrifying fully recommend this bar. As a straight women I wanted a place I could go have a drink and feel safe plus enjoy a Friday night out. This ticked all the boxes and to top it off the selection of drinks outstanding and the prices were fantastic too!

Seren Dippity

What a lovely LGBTQ+ venue!!! A bar that really cares about its punters and performers. Thank you for looking after us to beautifully.

Shanie Tolley

Patrik is an incredible host! He serves the most and Megan was absolutely amazing too! Will definitely be back here again soon made my birthday night incredible! Will be back soon!

Ben Jjd

LESBIANS Read this The Fox has completely lost what made it matter. It used to be a lesbian bar — one of the few places where women could actually feel comfortable, free, and among our own. Now it’s been taken over by men. They run it, they fill it, and they’ve pushed out the community that built it. It’s not a “mixed” space or “inclusive.” It’s just another bar for men that’s still trading off the reputation and history of lesbians. It’s insulting to call it a lesbian bar at this point. You can’t slap the word “queer” on the door and pretend it’s the same thing. What’s happening at The Fox is gentrification of lesbian space — cultural theft dressed up as progress. If you’re looking for a real lesbian bar, this isn’t it anymore.
