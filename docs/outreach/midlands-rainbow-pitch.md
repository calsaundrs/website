# Midlands Rainbow — guest-column / scene-update pitch

**Channel:** personal contact (DM or email — whichever you've actually used before)
**When to send:** any weekday morning. Avoid Friday afternoons.
**Personalisation needed:** their name, a recent piece of theirs you genuinely liked.

---

## Subject options (pick one)

- "A scene write-up for Midlands Rainbow?"
- "Birmingham nightlife update — fancy a guest piece?"

---

## Body — short version (use this first)

> Hey [name],
>
> Quick one — I run [Brum Outloud](https://brumoutloud.co.uk), the events + venue guide for Birmingham's LGBTQ+ scene. Been heads-down on it for a year and the scene has shifted a lot in that time: new nights at the Hub, Glamorous picking up steam, Pride 2026 just round the corner.
>
> I read your piece on [SPECIFIC PIECE — e.g. "the Wolverhampton drag scene"] and it's the closest thing to a real Midlands view I've seen anywhere. Brum doesn't get the coverage Manchester or Leeds do, and we both know it.
>
> Would Midlands Rainbow take a guest scene update from me? I can write the next 6 weeks of what's on, where the energy is, and what's worth getting the train for — Pride run-up included. Aim is around 800 words, ready to go.
>
> If a piece doesn't fit but you want me to send some scene info you can lift for your own write-up, even better — happy to be useful either way.
>
> Cheers,
> Cal
> [brumoutloud.co.uk](https://brumoutloud.co.uk)

---

## What to do if they say yes

- Pitch a piece title in your reply ("Birmingham's Gay Village in 2026 — a 6-week scene update")
- In the piece itself, link naturally to:
  - [/birmingham-pride](https://brumoutloud.co.uk/birmingham-pride) — the Pride guide
  - [/all-venues](https://brumoutloud.co.uk/all-venues) — the venue directory
  - 2–3 specific venue pages where you reference their venue (e.g. `/venue/the-hub` if you talk about Eden's events)
- Don't link to the homepage — venue/category pages convert better and the homepage is already getting the most traffic
- Aim for 3 contextual links, not 10. Anything more reads spammy and editors will strip it.

## What to do if they say no

- Thank them, keep the door open ("if a Pride round-up piece would land better, just say")
- The relationship is the asset; the link is a side effect

## What to do if they don't reply within a week

- One short nudge: *"No worries if it's not a fit — just bumping in case it got buried."*
- After that: leave it. Ask again next quarter when there's a new news peg.
