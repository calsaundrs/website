# Venue link request — template

**Channel:** Instagram DM is usually warmest for venue accounts. Email if they have a real "info@" address.
**When to send:** weekday afternoon — venues are awake by then, not yet swamped with the evening rush.
**Send to:** Missing Bar, The Fox, Eden Bar, The Hub at Eden, Nightingale Club, Equator Bar, Glamorous, Village Inn, Fountain Inn — one at a time, not a blast.

---

## The principle

Each venue page on Brum Outloud is a **free piece of marketing for them** — reviews, photos, upcoming events from their own listings. We're asking them to link to it from their website's About / Find Us / Press / Social section. Mutual benefit: their visitors get a curated list of what's on, our page picks up an authoritative backlink.

**Do not** sound like you're asking for SEO juice. You're not. You're sharing a useful page about their venue and offering to link both ways.

## What to do if they say yes

- Confirm where they'll put the link (footer / about / press)
- Once it's live, view-source check for `rel="nofollow"` — most CMSes default to nofollow on outbound; politely ask them to remove it if so. *"Quick one — looks like the link is set to nofollow, which means it doesn't count as an endorsement to search engines. Could you flip it to a normal link? Cheers."*

## What to do if they ghost

- Most will. That's fine — venue admin is chaotic.
- After 10 days, send: *"Bumping in case the DM got lost. No worries if not a fit."*
- After that, give them six months and try again. People change roles.

## What to do if they ask "what's the catch?"

> "No catch. We're independent and link to everyone — no listing fees, never will be. The only ask is the link helps your customers find you."

## Tracking

- Keep a simple spreadsheet: venue, sent date, replied (y/n), live link URL, follow-up date
- The first 3 wins are the hardest. Once a major venue links, the next ones find it easier to say yes.
